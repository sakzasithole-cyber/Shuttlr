Got Questions?

FREQUENTLY ASKED

When is Shuttlr launching? +

Shuttlr is targeting a launch in time for Rocking the Daisies 2026 (October 2026) in Cape Town. The web app and Android app on Google Play will launch simultaneously. Join the waitlist above to be notified the moment bookings go live.

Is Shuttlr free to use for passengers? +

Yes — downloading the app and creating an account is completely free. You only pay when you book a shuttle, and the price you see is the price you pay. No booking fees are added to the passenger price. Operators pay Shuttlr a 10% commission from what they earn.

What happens if the operator doesn't confirm my booking? +

Your payment is held in escrow and never touches the operator's account until they confirm your seat. If an operator does not respond within 24 hours, your full payment is automatically refunded — no forms, no waiting, no hassle. This is built into the core of how Shuttlr works.

How does Shuttlr verify shuttle operators? +

Every operator goes through a 5-step verification process before their listings go public: (1) Company registration with CIPC number, (2) Owner identity — SA ID document + selfie, (3) Document upload — CIPC cert, PDP / operator's licence, roadworthy certificates, (4) Driver registration — each driver submits their PDP and a photo, (5) Vehicle registration — each vehicle's plate, make and roadworthy cert is logged. Our team reviews all documents manually before approval.

Why do I need to verify my ID as a passenger? +

ID verification protects everyone. Drivers know exactly who is boarding their vehicle. It reduces no-shows, builds accountability and creates a safer environment for both sides. Your ID number and documents are reviewed only by Shuttlr admin — they are never shared with operators, drivers or other passengers. Only your name and profile photo are visible to drivers.

How much does it cost to list as an operator? +

Registration and listing are completely free. Shuttlr only earns when you earn — we deduct a 10% commission from each confirmed booking payment. There are no monthly fees, no upfront costs and no charge for listings that don't convert to bookings.

Which cities will Shuttlr operate in at launch? +

Shuttlr will launch in Cape Town and Johannesburg, covering events in the Western Cape and Gauteng regions. Durban and Pretoria are planned for Q4 2026. Operators from any city can register now to be ready for when their region goes live.

Is there an iOS app? +

At launch, Shuttlr will be available on Android (Google Play) and via the web app at shuttlr.co.za — which works perfectly on any iPhone browser. A native iOS app is planned for 2027.
